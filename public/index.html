Understand the pollutants

AirAware+ tracks common outdoor air pollutants. This information is for environmental awareness only and is not medical advice.

Tiny particles often linked to traffic, wood burning and industry. When levels are higher, they can worsen overall air quality and reduce visibility.

Common sources: exhaust fumes, smoke, construction dust.

Larger particles such as dust, pollen and brake/tyre wear. Higher levels often show up during dry, dusty conditions and near busy roads.

Common sources: road dust, construction, agriculture.

A gas strongly linked to traffic emissions. Levels tend to be higher near busy roads and can be lower in greener or less congested areas.

Common sources: diesel engines, gas boilers, power generation.

Forms in sunlight when other pollutants react. It can be higher on warm, bright days and sometimes peaks away from city centres.

Common conditions: sunny weather + stagnant air.

A gas linked to burning sulphur-containing fuels. Higher readings are more common near certain industrial sources or shipping routes.

Common sources: shipping, industrial combustion.

Produced by incomplete combustion. Outdoor levels are usually low, but can rise in heavy traffic or poorly ventilated areas near combustion sources.

Common sources: vehicle exhaust, combustion in enclosed/poorly ventilated spaces.
